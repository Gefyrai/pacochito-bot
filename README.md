## ABOUT PACOCHE LODGE & RESERVE

Pacoche Lodge & Reserve is located in one of the last
remaining semi-dry tropical forests on the Ecuadorian
coast, in the province of Manabí. The reserve covers
10ha within the Marine, Coastal and Wildlife Refuge
of 13,445ha. Located 20 minutes from Manta.
Home to howler monkeys, 250+ bird species (55 endemic),
bamboo, Shawl and Cady trees — origin of the Panama
hat and tagua (vegetable ivory).

Pacoche Lodge & Reserve is: MAGICAL, SUSTAINABLE
and UNIQUE.
Every visit contributes directly to the conservation
of the ecosystem and the howler monkey.

## WILDLIFE — HOWLER MONKEYS

Species: Alouatta palliata aequatorialis
Status: Endangered (IUCN Red List /
Libro Rojo Ecuador)
Every visit directly supports their conservation.

KEY FACTS:

- 40+ howler monkeys at Pacoche Lodge & Reserve
- 10+ family groups throughout the entire refuge
- Their howl — loudest sound made by any primate —
heard from over 1 kilometer away
- Nomadic: groups constantly change location —
every visit is a unique experience
- They move tree to tree feeding on higueron
leaves, bamboo, guarumo leaves, flowers, fruits
- Live in family groups led by 1-2 alpha males
- NOT aggressive — any reaction is purely
defensive of their habitat
- Also present: capuchin monkeys, ocelots,
kinkajous, squirrels, deer, 250+ bird species

BEST TIME FOR WILDLIFE SIGHTINGS:

- Early morning (from 08h00) = highest probability
- Midday = monkeys tend to rest and sleep
- Recommendation: always start as early as possible

HONEST EXPECTATION — MANDATORY GUARDRAIL:
When any visitor asks "Will we see monkeys?",
"Are there monkeys?" or "How many animals will
we see?", ALWAYS respond with this framing:

"Pacoche is home to rich thriving wildlife,
including 40+ howler monkeys in 10+ family groups.
However, as wild animals they roam freely — we
cannot guarantee sightings, and that's what makes
each visit truly unique! Probability is high,
especially early morning from 08h00 when they are
most active. You will almost certainly hear their
powerful howl echoing through the forest. Come
with open eyes, open ears, and an open heart —
Pacoche always surprises."
